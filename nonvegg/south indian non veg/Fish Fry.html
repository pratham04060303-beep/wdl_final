Fish Fry

Restaurant Style Recipe

Steps (Click for details)

1. Clean & Score the Fish
Clean fish pieces thoroughly under running water. Pat completely dry with paper towels. Make 2–3 deep diagonal cuts on each side of the fish so the marinade penetrates deep into the flesh for maximum flavour.
2. Marinate the Fish
In a bowl mix red chilli powder, turmeric, ginger-garlic paste, coriander powder, black pepper, fish masala, lemon juice and salt into a thick paste. Rub this marinade all over the fish and into the cuts generously. Rest for at least 30 minutes. Longer marination gives better flavour.
3. Coat with Flour
Dust the marinated fish lightly with rice flour or cornflour on both sides. This creates a thin crispy coating that seals in the juices and gives the fish fry its signature golden crust when fried.
4. Heat the Oil
Heat oil in a flat frying pan or tawa on medium-high heat. The oil should be hot enough — test by dropping a small piece of marinade in. If it sizzles immediately, the oil is ready. Use enough oil to shallow fry.
5. Fry the Fish
Gently slide the fish pieces into the hot oil. Fry on medium heat for 4–5 minutes without moving. Let a golden crust form before flipping. Flip carefully and fry the other side for another 4–5 minutes until golden, crispy and cooked through.
6. Fry Curry Leaves
In the same oil, fry a handful of fresh curry leaves for 30–40 seconds until crispy. These fried curry leaves are scattered over the fish fry and add an incredible aroma and crunch that is the signature finish of South Indian fish fry.
7. Serve Hot
Drain the fish on paper towels. Arrange on a plate and top with crispy fried curry leaves. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the top. Serve immediately with sliced onion rings and green chutney on the side.
