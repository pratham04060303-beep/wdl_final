Chettinad Chicken

Restaurant Style Recipe

Steps (Click for details)

1. Marinate the Chicken
Clean chicken pieces and marinate with turmeric, red chilli powder, ginger-garlic paste and salt. Mix well and rest for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour so the spices penetrate deep into the meat.
2. Dry Roast & Grind Spices
Dry roast black pepper, coriander seeds, cumin, fennel seeds, cloves, cinnamon and cardamom on low heat for 2–3 minutes until fragrant. Cool and grind to a fine powder. This freshly ground Chettinad masala is the heart of the dish.
3. Fry Onions & Tomatoes
Heat oil in a heavy pan. Add curry leaves and fry for 30 seconds. Add finely sliced onions and fry on medium heat until deep golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2 minutes. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until mushy and oil separates.
4. Add the Chettinad Masala
Add the freshly ground Chettinad spice powder along with red chilli powder, coriander powder, turmeric and garam masala. Mix well and cook the masala on medium heat for 3–4 minutes until oil rises to the surface.
5. Add Coconut
Add freshly grated coconut or coconut paste to the masala. Stir well and cook for 2–3 minutes. The coconut adds a subtle sweetness and richness that balances the intense spices of Chettinad cuisine beautifully.
6. Cook the Chicken
Add the marinated chicken pieces to the masala. Mix well so every piece is coated. Add a little water, cover and cook on medium-low heat for 20–25 minutes stirring occasionally until the chicken is fully cooked and the gravy is thick and coats the chicken.
7. Garnish & Serve
Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a few curry leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice, dosa, appam or parotta for a true Chettinad experience.
