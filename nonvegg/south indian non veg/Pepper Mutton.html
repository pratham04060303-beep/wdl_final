Pepper Mutton

Restaurant Style Recipe

1. Marinate the Mutton
Wash and clean the mutton pieces thoroughly. In a bowl, combine the mutton with salt, red chilli powder, half the crushed black pepper and garam masala. Mix well to coat every piece. Marinate for at least 30 minutes — or overnight in the fridge for deeper flavour. The pepper marinade begins tenderising the meat immediately.
2. Sear the Mutton
Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or pressure cooker over high heat. Add the marinated mutton pieces in a single layer and sear for 3–4 minutes on each side until browned all over. Do not stir too early — let it develop a crust. Remove and set aside. This searing step locks in juices and builds deep colour and flavour.
3. Build the Masala
In the same pan, reduce heat to medium. Add cumin seeds and fennel seeds and let them splutter for 30 seconds. Add finely sliced onion and cook for 7–8 minutes until deeply golden and caramelised. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Stir in tomato puree and cook for 4–5 minutes until the oil separates from the masala.
4. Pressure Cook the Mutton
Return the seared mutton to the pan. Add 200 ml of water and stir everything together. If using a pressure cooker, close the lid and cook on medium heat for 4–5 whistles until the mutton is completely tender and falling off the bone. If using a regular pot, cover and simmer on low heat for 60–75 minutes, checking occasionally and adding water if needed.
5. Finish with Pepper & Serve
Once the mutton is tender, open the lid and cook on high heat for 3–4 minutes to reduce any excess liquid until the masala coats the meat thickly. Add the remaining coarsely crushed black pepper and toss well — this final addition of fresh pepper gives the dish its signature bold, sharp heat. Garnish with fresh coriander and a squeeze of lemon. Serve hot with rice, roti or parotta.
