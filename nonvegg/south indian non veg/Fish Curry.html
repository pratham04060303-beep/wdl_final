South Indian Fish Curry

Restaurant Style Recipe

Steps (Click for details)

1. Marinate the Fish
Clean and wash the fish pieces thoroughly. Pat dry with a paper towel. Rub with salt and red chilli powder and set aside for 15–20 minutes. This firms up the fish slightly and helps it hold together during cooking without falling apart in the curry.
2. Soak the Tamarind
Soak a small lemon-sized ball of tamarind in 100 ml of warm water for 10 minutes. Squeeze and strain the pulp through your fingers to extract the tamarind juice. Discard the fibres and seeds. This tamarind water gives the curry its signature sour tang that defines South Indian fish curry.
3. Build the Curry Base
Heat oil in a wide pan or clay pot over medium heat. Add mustard or cumin seeds and let them splutter for 30 seconds. Add sliced onion and cook for 5–6 minutes until deep golden. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Stir in red chilli powder and cook for 30 seconds. Add tomato puree and cook for 4–5 minutes until the oil separates and the base is thick and dark.
4. Add Coconut Milk & Tamarind
Pour in the coconut milk and tamarind water. Stir well to combine everything into a smooth, rich sauce. Season with salt. Bring to a gentle simmer on medium-low heat for 5 minutes. The sauce should be a deep reddish-orange colour with a beautiful coconut aroma.
5. Cook the Fish & Serve
Gently slide the marinated fish pieces into the simmering curry. Do not stir — just spoon the sauce over the fish. Cook on low heat for 8–10 minutes until the fish is fully cooked through and flakes easily. Taste and adjust salt and sourness. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with steamed rice.
