1. Cook the Beef
Heat olive oil in a pan over high heat. Add minced beef and break it apart with a spoon. Cook for 6–8 minutes until fully browned. Add chopped onion and garlic and cook for 2–3 more minutes. Season with cumin, chilli flakes and salt. Stir in a few spoons of tomato salsa and cook for 2 minutes until thick. Set aside.
2. Layer the Nachos
Preheat oven to 200°C. Spread a single layer of nacho chips on a large baking tray or oven-safe dish. Spoon the cooked beef evenly over the chips. Grate or tear cheese generously over the top, making sure every chip gets some coverage.
3. Bake Until Melted
Place the tray in the preheated oven and bake for 8–10 minutes until the cheese is fully melted, bubbly and lightly golden at the edges. Watch closely — the chips can burn quickly. Remove as soon as the cheese looks gooey and perfect.
4. Top & Serve
Remove from the oven immediately. Spoon dollops of sour cream and salsa over the top. Serve straight from the tray while hot and crispy. Garnish with fresh coriander or jalapeños if available. Nachos must be eaten fresh — they go soggy quickly.
