Beef Chimichanga

1. Cook the Beef Filling
Heat a little oil in a pan over high heat. Add minced beef and cook for 6–8 minutes, breaking it up, until fully browned. Add chopped onion and garlic and cook for 3 more minutes. Season with cumin, chilli flakes and salt. Stir in the tomato salsa and simmer for 5 minutes until thick and dry. Let it cool slightly.
2. Warm the Tortillas
Warm the flour tortillas in a dry pan for 20–30 seconds per side until soft and pliable. Warm tortillas are essential — they will crack if folded cold.
3. Fill & Wrap
Lay a warm tortilla flat. Place a generous spoonful of beef filling in the centre. Top with shredded cheese. Fold the sides in, then roll tightly from the bottom up like a burrito. Secure with a toothpick if needed to hold the shape during frying.
4. Fry the Chimichanga
Heat oil in a deep pan to 180°C. Carefully lower the wrapped chimichanga seam-side down into the oil. Fry for 2–3 minutes per side until deep golden brown and crispy all over. Drain on paper towels. Alternatively, brush with oil and bake at 200°C for 20 minutes, flipping halfway.
5. Serve
Remove the toothpick. Place the chimichanga on a plate and top with a dollop of sour cream and a spoonful of salsa. Serve immediately while hot and crispy. Garnish with fresh coriander if available.
