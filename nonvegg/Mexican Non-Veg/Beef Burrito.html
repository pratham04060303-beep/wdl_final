Beef Burrito

1. Cook the Rice & Beans
Cook rice as per packet instructions and set aside. In a separate pan, heat a little olive oil and warm the drained kidney beans with a pinch of cumin, salt and chilli flakes. Mash lightly with a fork to create a thick, chunky texture. Keep warm.
2. Season & Brown the Beef
Season the minced beef generously with salt, black pepper, cumin and chilli flakes. Heat olive oil in a large pan over high heat. Add the beef and cook for 6–8 minutes, breaking it apart with a spoon, until browned and cooked through with no pink remaining.
3. Make the Beef Filling
Reduce heat to medium. Add finely chopped onion, garlic and diced capsicum to the browned beef. Cook for 3–4 minutes until softened. Stir in tomato puree and cook for another 3 minutes until the mixture is rich, thick and well combined. Squeeze in a little lemon juice and adjust seasoning.
4. Warm the Tortillas
Warm the flour tortillas one at a time in a dry pan over medium heat for 20–30 seconds per side until soft, pliable and lightly toasted. Alternatively, wrap them in a damp cloth and microwave for 30 seconds. Warm tortillas fold without cracking.
5. Assemble the Burrito
Lay a warm tortilla flat. Layer in the centre: a scoop of rice, a spoonful of kidney beans, a generous portion of the beef filling, shredded cheese, a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkle of fresh coriander. Do not overfill — leave at least 5 cm of space on each side for folding.
6. Fold & Wrap
Fold the two sides of the tortilla inward over the filling. Then fold the bottom edge up tightly over the filling and roll firmly forward to seal into a tight burrito. Place seam-side down. For a crispy finish, toast the wrapped burrito in a hot pan for 1–2 minutes per side until golden and sealed.
7. Slice & Serve
Cut the burrito in half diagonally and serve immediately. Pair with extra salsa, sour cream, guacamole or hot sauce on the side. Garnish with fresh coriander and a wedge of lemon for a bright, zesty finish.
