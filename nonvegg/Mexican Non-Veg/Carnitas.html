1. Season the Pork
Cut the pork shoulder or belly into large chunks (about 5 cm). In a large bowl, rub the pork generously with salt, black pepper, cumin and chilli flakes. Add minced garlic and a good squeeze of lemon or orange juice. Mix well and marinate for at least 30 minutes, or overnight in the fridge for deeper flavour.
2. Slow Cook the Pork
Heat olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the pork chunks on all sides for 2–3 minutes until browned. Add chopped onion, garlic and enough water to come halfway up the pork. Cover and cook on low heat for 2–2.5 hours until the pork is completely tender and falling apart.
3. Shred & Crisp the Pork
Remove the pork from the pot and shred it with two forks into chunky pulled pieces. Spread the shredded pork on a baking tray in a single layer. Place under a grill or in an oven at 220°C for 8–10 minutes until the edges are crispy and caramelised. This step creates the signature crispy-yet-juicy carnitas texture.
4. Warm the Tortillas
Warm the flour tortillas in a dry pan for 20–30 seconds per side until soft and pliable. Keep them wrapped in a clean cloth to stay warm while you assemble.
5. Assemble & Serve
Pile a generous portion of crispy carnitas into each warm tortilla. Top with tomato salsa, fresh coriander and a squeeze of lemon. Serve immediately while the pork is hot and crispy. Carnitas also work beautifully served over rice or nachos.
