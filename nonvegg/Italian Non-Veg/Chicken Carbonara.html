Chicken Carbonara

Steps (Click for details)

1. Boil the Pasta
Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti or pasta until al dente as per packet instructions. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining — this starchy water is key to making the sauce creamy.
2. Cook the Chicken
Season chicken pieces with salt and black pepper. Heat olive oil and butter in a pan. Cook chicken on medium-high heat for 5–6 minutes on each side until golden and cooked through. Slice into strips and set aside.
3. Sauté the Garlic
In the same pan on low heat, add a little butter and sauté minced garlic for 1–2 minutes until fragrant and lightly golden. Do not burn the garlic as it will turn bitter.
4. Make the Carbonara Sauce
In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, fresh cream and grated parmesan cheese. Add a generous amount of black pepper. This mixture forms the rich, creamy carbonara sauce.
5. Combine Pasta & Sauce
Remove the pan from heat. Add the drained pasta to the garlic pan. Quickly pour the egg-cream-cheese mixture over the pasta and toss rapidly. Add reserved pasta water little by little to loosen the sauce to a silky creamy consistency.
6. Add the Chicken
Add the sliced cooked chicken into the creamy pasta. Toss gently to combine everything evenly. The residual heat will finish cooking the sauce without scrambling the eggs.
7. Garnish & Serve
Plate immediately and garnish with extra parmesan, freshly cracked black pepper and chopped parsley. Serve hot right away as carbonara tastes best fresh.
