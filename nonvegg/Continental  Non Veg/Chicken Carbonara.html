1. Cook the Pasta
Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add a generous amount of salt — it should taste like the sea. Cook the spaghetti according to packet instructions until al dente (firm to the bite). Before draining, reserve at least 1 full cup of the starchy pasta cooking water — this is essential for building the sauce. Drain and set aside.
2. Cook the Chicken
Season the chicken strips with salt and coarsely crushed black pepper. Heat olive oil and butter in a wide pan over medium-high heat. Cook the chicken for 4–5 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute until fragrant. Remove from heat and slice if needed. Set aside in the pan.
3. Make the Carbonara Sauce
In a bowl whisk together 2 whole eggs and 2 egg yolks with the grated Parmesan cheese, a splash of fresh cream and a generous amount of coarsely crushed black pepper. Whisk until completely smooth and glossy. This egg-cheese mixture IS the carbonara sauce — do not add heat to it directly or it will scramble.
4. Combine — The Critical Step
Remove the chicken pan from heat completely and let it cool for 30 seconds — this is crucial. Add the drained hot pasta to the pan and toss well with the chicken and its juices. Now pour the egg-cheese sauce over the pasta and toss rapidly, adding the reserved pasta water a little at a time to loosen the sauce. The residual heat from the pasta cooks the eggs gently into a silky, creamy coating without scrambling. Keep tossing until every strand is coated in a glossy sauce.
5. Plate & Serve
Taste and adjust salt. Twirl the pasta into bowls using tongs. Arrange the chicken pieces on top. Finish with an extra generous grating of Parmesan, a heavy crack of black pepper and fresh parsley. Serve immediately — carbonara waits for no one and thickens quickly as it cools.
