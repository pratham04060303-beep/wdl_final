Chicken Cacciatore

1. Season & Brown the Chicken
Pat chicken pieces dry with paper towels. Season generously with salt and black pepper on all sides. Heat olive oil in a large heavy pan or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the chicken pieces for 4–5 minutes per side until deeply golden. Remove and set aside — this browning builds the flavour base of the dish.
2. Sauté Onion & Garlic
In the same pan with the remaining oil, add sliced onion and cook on medium heat for 4–5 minutes until softened and golden. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute. Scrape up the browned bits from the pan — these are full of flavour.
3. Add Bell Peppers & Mushrooms
Add sliced bell peppers and mushrooms to the pan. Cook on medium-high heat for 4–5 minutes stirring occasionally until they soften and develop a little colour. The vegetables form the body of the rich cacciatore sauce.
4. Deglaze with Wine
Pour in white wine or chicken stock and stir well. Let it bubble vigorously for 2–3 minutes until reduced by half. This deglazes the pan and adds a deep, complex flavour to the sauce that makes cacciatore so special.
5. Add Tomatoes & Herbs
Add crushed tomatoes, chilli flakes, dried oregano and fresh basil. Stir everything together. Season with salt and black pepper. Bring to a gentle simmer and let the sauce cook for 5 minutes until it starts to thicken and become fragrant.
6. Braise the Chicken
Nestle the browned chicken pieces back into the pan, skin side up. The sauce should come halfway up the chicken. Cover with a lid and cook on low heat for 30–35 minutes until the chicken is fall-off-the-bone tender and the sauce is rich and thick.
7. Garnish & Serve
Taste and adjust seasoning. Scatter fresh basil leaves on top. Serve hot with crusty bread, pasta, polenta or steamed rice to soak up all the incredible sauce.
