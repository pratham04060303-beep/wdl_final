1. Season & Sear the Chicken
Pat the chicken pieces completely dry with paper towels — this is essential for a good sear. Season generously all over with salt and black pepper. Heat olive oil and butter in a large wide pan or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear the chicken pieces skin-side down for 4–5 minutes without moving until deep golden brown. Flip and sear the other side for 3 minutes. Remove and set aside — the chicken will finish cooking in the sauce.
2. Sauté the Aromatics & Mushrooms
In the same pan with all the chicken drippings, reduce heat to medium. Add finely chopped onion and cook for 4–5 minutes until soft and translucent. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the sliced button mushrooms and cook for 4–5 minutes until golden and all their moisture has evaporated. Season with salt and black pepper.
3. Deglaze & Build the Sauce
Pour in the white wine or white wine vinegar and stir, scraping up all the golden bits from the bottom of the pan — this is pure flavour. Let it bubble and reduce for 2 minutes. Pour in the chicken stock and stir to combine. Bring to a gentle simmer. The sauce base should smell rich and deeply savoury at this point.
4. Braise the Chicken
Return the seared chicken pieces to the pan, nestling them into the sauce. The liquid should come about halfway up the chicken. Cover with a lid and simmer on low heat for 25–30 minutes, turning the chicken once halfway through, until fully cooked through and completely tender. The sauce will reduce and deepen in flavour as it cooks.
5. Finish with Cream & Serve
Remove the chicken pieces and set aside. Stir the fresh cream into the sauce and simmer uncovered for 3–4 minutes until it thickens to a velvety, glossy consistency. Taste and adjust salt and pepper. Return the chicken to the pan and spoon the sauce over it. Garnish generously with chopped fresh parsley. Serve immediately with crusty bread, mashed potato or steamed rice.
