Egg Kathi Roll

1. Prep the Onion Filling
Thinly slice the onion and toss with a pinch of salt, red chilli powder, cumin powder, a squeeze of lemon juice and fresh coriander. Mix well and set aside — this quick-pickled onion mixture is the heart of a great kathi roll, adding crunch, tang and heat to every bite.
2. Warm the Paratha
Heat a tawa or flat pan over medium heat. Brush with a little oil or butter. Place the paratha or wrap on the tawa and warm for 20–30 seconds per side until soft, slightly golden and pliable. Keep warm wrapped in a clean cloth while you cook the egg.
3. Make the Egg Omelette on the Paratha
Beat 2 eggs in a bowl with a pinch of salt, red chilli powder, black pepper and chopped coriander. Add a little oil to the hot tawa. Pour the beaten egg directly onto the tawa in a circle roughly the size of the paratha. Immediately place the warm paratha on top of the egg, pressing down gently. Let the egg cook and stick to the paratha for 1–2 minutes until set. Flip together so the egg side faces up — golden, spiced and bonded to the bread. This is the signature kathi roll technique.
4. Assemble & Roll
With the egg side facing up, spread a layer of green chutney and sour cream or mayo across the centre. Pile the spiced pickled onion filling on top. Add a final squeeze of lemon and a sprinkle of fresh coriander. Roll tightly from one side, pressing firmly as you go. Wrap the lower half in foil or paper to hold it together. Serve immediately while hot.
