1. Prepare the Mince
Use finely minced mutton or chicken with some fat content for juicy kebabs. If the mince is too wet, spread it on a cloth and squeeze out excess moisture. Dry mince is essential — wet mince will fall off the skewer during cooking.
2. Mix the Kebab Mixture
In a large bowl combine the mince with finely chopped onion, ginger-garlic paste, chopped green chilli, red chilli powder, garam masala, coriander powder, turmeric, black pepper, fresh coriander and salt. Mix thoroughly with your hands for 3–4 minutes until the mixture is smooth and holds together well.
3. Rest the Mixture
Cover the kebab mixture and refrigerate for at least 1–2 hours or overnight. This resting time allows the spices to bloom into the meat and helps the mixture firm up so it stays on the skewer without breaking.
4. Shape on Skewers
Wet your hands with water to prevent sticking. Take a portion of the mixture and mould it firmly around a metal skewer in a long sausage shape about 10–12 cm long. Press firmly at both ends to seal. The mixture should grip the skewer tightly without gaps.
5. Grill or Cook on Tawa
For grilling: cook on a hot grill or BBQ for 8–10 minutes turning every 2 minutes until charred and cooked through. For tawa: heat a cast iron tawa with a little oil and cook the kebabs on medium-high heat turning regularly for 10–12 minutes until browned all around.
6. Baste with Butter
During the last 2–3 minutes of cooking, baste the kebabs generously with melted butter. This gives them a rich glossy finish and the signature smoky buttery flavour. You can also hold them over a gas flame briefly for extra char.
7. Serve Hot
Slide the kebabs off the skewer onto a plate. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over them. Serve immediately with mint chutney, sliced onion rings, lemon wedges and roomali roti or naan.
