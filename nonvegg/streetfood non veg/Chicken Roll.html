Street Food Chicken Roll

1. Marinate the Chicken
Slice the boneless chicken into thin strips. In a bowl mix with red chilli powder, cumin powder, garam masala, black pepper, garlic, a squeeze of lemon juice, salt and a drizzle of oil. Mix well and marinate for at least 20–30 minutes. The longer it marinates the more flavourful and tender the chicken will be.
2. Cook the Chicken
Heat oil in a pan or tawa over high heat. Add the marinated chicken strips in a single layer and cook for 3–4 minutes per side without disturbing, until nicely charred on the edges and cooked through. The high heat creates those irresistible smoky street-style char marks. Squeeze a little lemon juice over at the end and toss. Set aside.
3. Caramelise the Onions
In the same pan, add a little oil and cook the sliced onions on high heat for 3–4 minutes until lightly charred and caramelised at the edges. Season with a pinch of salt and black pepper. These smoky onions are a key part of the street-roll flavour — do not skip this step.
4. Warm the Paratha / Wrap
Heat the paratha or wrap on a dry tawa over medium heat for 20–30 seconds per side until warm, soft and slightly toasted. You can also brush with a little oil or butter for extra richness just like street vendors do.
5. Assemble & Roll
Lay the warm paratha flat. Spread a generous layer of green chutney or sauce and a spoonful of sour cream or mayo down the centre. Layer the cooked chicken strips and charred onions on top. Add fresh coriander and a final squeeze of lemon. Roll tightly, wrap the bottom half in foil or paper to hold it together. Serve immediately while hot and fresh.
