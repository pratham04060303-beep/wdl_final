Egg Bhurji

Steps (Click for details)

1. Beat the Eggs
Crack the eggs into a bowl. Add a pinch of salt and beat well until the yolks and whites are fully combined. Set aside. Beating properly gives the bhurji a light and fluffy texture when cooked.
2. Heat Butter & Oil
Heat a pan or tawa on medium flame. Add butter and a little oil together. Using both gives rich flavour from the butter and prevents it from burning. Let the butter melt completely and start to sizzle.
3. Sauté Onion & Green Chilli
Add finely chopped onion and slit green chillies to the pan. Sauté on medium heat for 3–4 minutes until the onions turn soft and lightly golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 more minute until fragrant.
4. Add Tomatoes & Spices
Add finely chopped tomatoes along with red chilli powder, turmeric and salt. Cook on medium heat for 3–4 minutes until the tomatoes turn soft and mushy and the masala comes together with the oil slightly separating.
5. Pour in the Eggs
Reduce the heat to low-medium. Pour the beaten eggs over the masala. Let it sit for 10–15 seconds before stirring. This allows the eggs to start setting slightly before you scramble them into the masala.
6. Scramble & Cook
Gently scramble the eggs using a spatula in large soft folds, mixing them with the masala. Keep stirring on low-medium heat for 2–3 minutes until the eggs are just cooked and moist. Do not overcook — remove from heat while still slightly soft as they continue cooking in residual heat.
7. Garnish & Serve
Sprinkle garam masala and a generous amount of freshly chopped coriander leaves on top. Serve hot immediately with buttered pav, roti, paratha or toasted bread. Bhurji tastes best fresh off the pan.
