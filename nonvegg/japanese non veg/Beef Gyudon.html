Beef Gyudon

1. Cook the Steamed Rice
Wash rice thoroughly until the water runs clear. Cook using a rice cooker or pot with a 1:1.2 ratio of rice to water. Keep warm while preparing the beef topping. Japanese short grain rice is ideal but basmati works well too.
2. Prepare the Gyudon Sauce
In a bowl mix together soy sauce, mirin, sake or rice wine vinegar, sugar and a little water. This sweet-savoury tare sauce is the soul of gyudon. Adjust sweetness and saltiness to your taste.
3. Cook the Onions
Heat a pan over medium heat. Add a little oil. Add thinly sliced onion and cook for 4–5 minutes until softened and lightly golden. Add grated ginger and cook for 1 more minute until fragrant.
4. Add the Sauce
Pour the prepared gyudon sauce over the cooked onions. Stir well and bring to a gentle simmer. Let it cook for 2–3 minutes so the onions absorb all the sweet and savoury flavours of the sauce.
5. Cook the Beef
Add the thinly sliced beef into the simmering sauce and onions. Spread the slices evenly and cook on medium heat for 2–3 minutes until the beef is just cooked through and has absorbed the sauce. Do not overcook or the beef will toughen.
6. Soft Boil the Egg
Bring water to boil. Gently lower the egg and cook for exactly 6–7 minutes for a soft runny yolk. Transfer immediately to cold water. Peel carefully. The soft yolk adds a rich creamy element when broken over the hot gyudon.
7. Assemble & Serve
Scoop steamed rice into a bowl. Ladle the beef and onion mixture generously on top with all the sauce. Place the soft boiled egg on top. Garnish with sliced spring onion greens and a pinch of black pepper. Serve immediately.
