Prawn Gyoza

Restaurant Style Recipe

1. Make the Prawn Filling
Finely chop the peeled and deveined prawns into a coarse mince — do not blend smooth, a little texture is ideal. Transfer to a bowl and mix with minced garlic, soy sauce, black pepper, salt, chilli flakes and finely chopped fresh coriander. Stir well until combined. Refrigerate for 10 minutes to firm up.
2. Fill the Gyoza
Lay a gyoza wrapper flat on your palm. Place 1 heaped teaspoon of prawn filling in the centre — do not overfill. Dip your finger in water and wet the edge of the wrapper all the way around. Fold the wrapper in half over the filling to form a half-moon. Pinch the edges firmly to seal, then make small pleats along the sealed edge to create the classic gyoza shape.
3. Pan-Fry the Gyoza
Heat oil in a flat-bottomed non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Place the gyoza flat-side down in a single layer without touching. Fry for 2–3 minutes until the bottoms are golden brown and crispy. Do not move them while frying.
4. Steam to Finish
Once the bottoms are golden, carefully pour 3–4 tablespoons of water into the pan — it will sizzle and steam up instantly. Immediately cover with a lid and steam on medium heat for 3–4 minutes until the water evaporates and the gyoza wrappers are cooked through and slightly translucent. Remove the lid and let any remaining water evaporate for 30 seconds.
5. Serve
Transfer gyoza to a plate crispy-side up. Mix soy sauce with a squeeze of lemon and chilli flakes as a simple dipping sauce. Serve immediately while hot and crispy. Garnish with fresh coriander. Gyoza must be eaten right away — they lose their crunch if left to sit.
