Tuna Sashimi

1. Source & Chill the Tuna
Always use sashimi-grade or sushi-grade tuna — this means it has been handled and frozen specifically to be safe for raw consumption. Buy from a trusted fishmonger. Keep the tuna refrigerated and only take it out right before slicing. A very cold fish is much easier to slice cleanly and thinly.
2. Prepare the Tuna Block
Pat the tuna fillet dry with paper towels. Inspect it and remove any visible sinew or dark bloodline with a sharp knife — these have a strong taste. Place the tuna on a clean cutting board. Identify the grain of the fish (the direction the muscle fibres run) — you will always cut against the grain for sashimi.
3. Slice the Sashimi
Use a long, very sharp knife — ideally a Japanese sashimi knife or a thin carving knife. In one smooth, single pulling motion (do not saw), slice the tuna against the grain into pieces about 5–7 mm thick. Each slice should be clean and smooth with no tearing. Wipe the blade with a damp cloth between each cut for clean slices.
4. Plate & Serve
Arrange the tuna slices in a neat fan or overlapping row on a chilled plate. Place a small mound of wasabi paste and a few slices of pickled ginger on the side. Add a wedge of lemon. Garnish with microgreens or fresh coriander. Pour soy sauce into a small dipping bowl alongside. Serve immediately — sashimi must always be eaten fresh and ice cold.
