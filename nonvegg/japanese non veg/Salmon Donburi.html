Salmon Donburi

Restaurant Style Recipe

Steps (Click for details)

1. Cook the Steamed Rice
Wash rice thoroughly until the water runs clear. Cook using a rice cooker or pot. Once cooked, season with a little rice wine vinegar, sugar and salt while still warm. Fluff gently and keep covered until ready to serve.
2. Make the Donburi Sauce
Mix together soy sauce, mirin, sake or rice wine vinegar, a pinch of sugar and grated ginger in a small bowl. This sweet-savoury glaze will coat the salmon beautifully. Taste and adjust to your preference.
3. Prepare the Salmon
Pat the salmon fillet dry with paper towels. Season both sides with salt and black pepper. If using fresh sashimi-grade salmon, you can serve it raw — slice into clean pieces. For cooked salmon, proceed to the next step.
4. Sear the Salmon
Heat sesame oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Place salmon skin-side down and sear for 3–4 minutes until the skin is crispy. Flip and cook for 2 more minutes until the flesh is just cooked through and flakes easily. Do not overcook.
5. Glaze with Sauce
Pour the donburi sauce over the salmon in the pan. Let it bubble and caramelise for 1–2 minutes, spooning the sauce over the fish continuously so it gets a glossy, flavourful coating all over.
6. Prepare the Toppings
Slice avocado into thin pieces. Chop spring onion greens finely. These fresh toppings add creaminess and freshness that balance the rich soy-glazed salmon perfectly.
7. Assemble & Serve
Scoop warm seasoned rice into a bowl. Place the glazed salmon on top. Arrange sliced avocado on the side. Drizzle any remaining sauce from the pan over everything. Garnish with spring onion, a drizzle of sesame oil and serve immediately.
