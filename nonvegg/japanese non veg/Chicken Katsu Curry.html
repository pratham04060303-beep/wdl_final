Chicken Katsu Curry

1. Prepare the Chicken
Place chicken breasts on a chopping board. Cover with cling film and pound with a rolling pin or meat mallet to an even thickness of about 1.5 cm. Season both sides well with salt, black pepper and a splash of soy sauce. Let it sit for 10 minutes to absorb the seasoning.
2. Bread the Chicken (Katsu)
Set up a breading station: one plate of plain flour, one bowl of beaten egg, one plate of panko breadcrumbs. Coat each chicken breast first in flour (shake off excess), then dip in egg, then press firmly into panko breadcrumbs on both sides until fully and evenly coated. Rest for 5 minutes so the coating sticks.
3. Fry the Katsu
Heat oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat. The oil should be about 1 cm deep. Carefully place the breaded chicken in the pan and fry for 4–5 minutes per side until deep golden brown and crispy. The chicken should be fully cooked through with no pink inside. Drain on paper towels and rest for 2 minutes before slicing.
4. Make the Curry Sauce
In a separate saucepan, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add chopped onion and garlic and cook for 4–5 minutes until soft and lightly golden. Add 400 ml of water and bring to a boil. Break in the Japanese curry roux blocks and stir until completely dissolved. Simmer on low heat for 8–10 minutes, stirring often, until the sauce is thick, glossy and rich.
5. Slice & Serve
Slice the fried chicken katsu diagonally into thick strips. Place over a mound of steamed rice. Ladle the Japanese curry sauce generously over the chicken and rice. Serve immediately while the katsu is still crispy. Garnish with pickled ginger or fresh coriander if available.
