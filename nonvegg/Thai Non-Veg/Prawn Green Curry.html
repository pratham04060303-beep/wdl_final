Prawn green curry

Restaurant Style Recipe

1. Prepare the Prawns
Peel and devein the prawns, leaving the tails on if desired. Pat them dry with a paper towel. Season with salt, black pepper and a squeeze of lemon juice. Set aside. Keep the shells to make a quick stock if desired.
2. Warm the Stock
Pour the vegetable or seafood stock into a saucepan and keep it warm on low heat throughout cooking. Having warm stock is essential — adding cold stock to the risotto stops the cooking process and ruins the texture.
3. Sauté the Base
In a wide, heavy-bottomed pan, heat olive oil and 1 tbsp butter over medium heat. Add finely chopped onion and cook for 4–5 minutes until soft and translucent. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute until fragrant.
4. Toast the Rice
Add the Arborio rice to the pan and stir well to coat each grain in the butter and oil. Toast the rice for 1–2 minutes until the edges turn slightly translucent. This step builds the nutty base flavour of the risotto.
5. Add Wine & Cook the Risotto
Pour in the white wine or white wine vinegar and stir until absorbed. Begin adding the warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring continuously and waiting until each ladle is fully absorbed before adding the next. Repeat for 18–20 minutes until the rice is creamy and al dente.
6. Cook the Prawns
In a separate pan, heat butter over high heat. Sear the seasoned prawns for 1–2 minutes per side until pink, curled and lightly golden. Do not overcook. Set aside. You can also add them directly to the risotto in the last 3 minutes of cooking.
7. Finish & Serve
Remove the risotto from heat. Stir in the remaining butter, a splash of cream and grated Parmesan cheese. Fold in the cooked prawns gently. Season with salt, black pepper and a squeeze of lemon. Serve immediately in warm bowls topped with fresh parsley and extra Parmesan.
