Thai Fish Cakes

Restaurant Style Recipe

1. Prepare the Fish Paste
Pat the fish fillets dry with a paper towel. Chop into rough chunks and add to a food processor or blender. Add Thai red curry paste, garlic, soy sauce, chilli flakes, salt and black pepper. Blend into a smooth, thick paste. Do not over-blend — a slightly coarse texture gives better texture in the cakes.
2. Mix & Shape
Transfer the fish paste to a bowl. Fold in finely chopped fresh coriander or Thai basil. Mix well. Wet your hands slightly and shape the mixture into small round flat patties about 1.5 cm thick and 6 cm wide. You should get 8–10 cakes. Place on a plate and refrigerate for 15–20 minutes to firm up.
3. Fry the Fish Cakes
Heat oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, carefully place the fish cakes in the pan — do not overcrowd. Fry for 3–4 minutes per side until deep golden brown and cooked through. The outside should be crispy and the inside firm and springy. Drain on paper towels.
4. Serve
Arrange the fish cakes on a plate. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over them. Serve immediately with sweet chilli sauce or dipping sauce on the side. Garnish with extra coriander or basil. Best eaten hot and fresh while the outside is still crispy.
