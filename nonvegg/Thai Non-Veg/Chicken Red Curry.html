Thai Chicken Red Curry

Restaurant Style Recipe

Steps (Click for details)

1. Prepare the Chicken
Cut chicken into bite-sized pieces. Season lightly with salt and red chilli powder. Set aside. If using chicken with bones, curry-cut pieces add more flavour to the sauce as they cook.
2. Fry the Curry Paste
Heat olive oil in a wide pan or wok over medium heat. Add sliced onion and cook for 3 minutes until soft. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Spoon in 2–3 tablespoons of Thai red curry paste and fry for 2–3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the paste is fragrant and darkens slightly in colour.
3. Add Chicken & Simmer
Add the seasoned chicken pieces to the pan and stir well to coat in the curry paste. Cook on medium-high heat for 5–6 minutes until the chicken is lightly seared on the outside. Pour in the coconut milk and stir to combine. Bring to a gentle boil then reduce to a simmer.
4. Cook the Curry
Simmer the curry on medium-low heat for 15–18 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is fully cooked through and the sauce has thickened to a rich, creamy consistency. Taste and adjust salt and chilli. Stir in a splash of fresh cream for extra richness if desired.
5. Finish & Serve
Remove from heat and squeeze in a little lemon juice to brighten the flavours. Ladle the curry over steamed rice. Garnish with fresh coriander or Thai basil leaves. Serve hot immediately for the best flavour and aroma.
